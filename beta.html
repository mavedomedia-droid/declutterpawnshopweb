What we're building

DECLUTTERING IS ALREADY A HABIT.
WE'RE MAKING IT WORK BETTER.

Nigerians already sell phones, furniture, clothes, electronics every day — on WhatsApp, Instagram, and anywhere people are listening. The behaviour is there. The problem is everything around it: awkward payments, strangers who ghost you, no structure, and zero trust on both sides.

Our goal with DPS is simple — encourage people to turn idle items into cash, free up space, and do it safely. In this economy, that matters. An item sitting in your house doing nothing could be someone else's need and your extra income.

We built the structure around it. You list, we post it, buyer pays via Paystack, funds are held in escrow, you meet, buyer confirms, you get paid. Everything tracked. Our Instagram is for brand content — items drop on Stories. Telegram is where the buying happens.

Why it's safe

TRUST WAS THE
MISSING PIECE.

The biggest problem with selling pre-owned in Nigeria wasn't demand — it was trust. Buyers worried about paying and not getting what was described. Sellers worried about getting scammed or shortchanged.

We built escrow into the core of DPS. The buyer's money is held securely from the moment they pay — the seller can't access it until the transaction is confirmed. Then we went a step further: PIN verification.

When a buyer pays, they receive a 4-digit PIN by email. Only they have it. The seller only gets paid when the buyer physically gives them that PIN at pickup — which only happens if they're satisfied with the item. Both sides are protected. No leap of faith required.
